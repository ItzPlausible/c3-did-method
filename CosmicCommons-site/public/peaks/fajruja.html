Investment Thesis

America faces a housing crisis driven by decades of underbuilding, rising material costs, and skilled labor shortages. Construction productivity has remained flat for 50 years while housing costs have outpaced wage growth in virtually every metropolitan area.

Fajruja addresses this crisis through technology that dramatically reduces construction costs while building cooperative wealth for both construction workers and future residents. The dual DACO model aligns builder and resident interests, creating communities where workers have a stake in long-term housing quality.

Construction Robotics

3D Printed Structures

Large-scale additive manufacturing for foundation, wall, and utility systems that can reduce build times by 70% while using sustainable, locally-sourced materials.

Modular Systems

Factory-built housing components that can be assembled rapidly on-site, enabling year-round construction and consistent quality regardless of weather conditions.

Community Land Trusts

Integrated CLT structures that preserve housing affordability permanently, removing speculative pressure while enabling residents to build equity through cooperative ownership.

E2C Pathway

Fajruja's dual DACO model creates two parallel cooperative structures: worker cooperatives that own and operate construction technology, and housing cooperatives that own the communities built. Workers who help construct a community can become residents, aligning incentives for quality and long-term durability.

This model directly addresses the housing affordability crisis by removing developer profit margins and speculative appreciation from housing costs, while creating dignified employment and ownership opportunities for construction workers.
